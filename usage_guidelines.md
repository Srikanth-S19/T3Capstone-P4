### Guidelines for Responsible Use of Churn Prediction API Outputs

The churn prediction API must be used strictly within predefined operational boundaries. If not, it can lead to algorithmic discrimination, margin erosion, or alienated customer bases.

#### How the API Output SHOULD Be Used

- **Proactive Retention Incentives:** Use probability scores to trigger targeted, low-cost marketing workflows (e.g., automated cart-recovery reminders or lifecycle replenishment emails) for users who cross the optimized risk threshold ($\pi = 0.2$).

- **Service & Support Recovery Priority:** High-risk scores paired with unresolved support logs should be used to route customer profiles directly to a high-touch, human concierge support desk. The goal is to solve operational friction (such as delayed refunds or product complaints) rather than pushing more marketing material.

- **Optimized Resource and Budget Allocation:** Use the predictions to allocate promotional capital efficiently. Direct high-value retention budgets (like significant store credits or personalized win-back offers) toward slipping, high-value accounts while suppressing spend on naturally secure or long-detached cohorts.

- **A/B Testing and Strategic Validation:** Treat the predictive output as an experimental baseline. Continuously run randomized trials (RCTs) on a small subset of flagged users to measure the actual lift and incremental return on investment (ROI) generated by your retention campaigns.

#### How the API Output SHOULD NOT Be Used

- **Algorithmic Price Discrimination:** Do not use high retention probabilities to penalize loyal customers by hiking baseline prices or reducing standard access to rewards ("loyalty penalty"). Also, avoid trapping volatile shoppers in a loop of endless, margin-diluting discounts.

- **Automated VIP Account Termination:** A high churn prediction should never result in automated account suspension, blacklisting, or the unprompted stripping of earned loyalty statuses or accumulated perks.

- **Un-Consented PII Marketing Blasts:** Never cross-reference the digital web tracking signals used by the inference engine to target users who have explicitly opted out of marketing activities (`marketing_consent == 'No'`). Privacy compliance must always override algorithmic prediction models.
